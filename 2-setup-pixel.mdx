# Setting Up Your Tracking Pixel

Alright, now that we have your integrations connected, the next thing we’re going to do is set up our pixel. This is the key to getting that 100% tracking accuracy, so let’s dive in.

## Enable Ad Tracking

First things first, head over to the **Ad Tracking** page in the sidebar. If this is your first time here, you might see a button to turn it on—go ahead and click that to activate ad tracking for your account.

Once that's active, navigate to the **Tracking Pixels** tab.

## Improve Tracking Accuracy

Before we create the pixel, let's talk about accuracy. By default, most tracking is "third-party," which browsers like Safari and Brave often block. To bypass these privacy blockers, we're going to set up a **Custom Tracking Domain**.

<Note>**Why do this?** A custom subdomain makes your pixel load as a first-party script. Without this, you’ll get about 98% accuracy. With it, you’re hitting 100%.</Note>

### Setting Up Your Custom Subdomain

1. Click **Set Up Now** under "Improve Tracking Accuracy."
2. Enter a subdomain (e.g., use.yourdomain.com) and click **Add Domain**.
3. You’ll be prompted to add two DNS records to your domain provider:
    - **CNAME Record:** Pointing to our tracking engine.
    - **TXT Record:** For verification.
4. Once added, click **Verify Domain**.

## Creating and Installing Your Pixel

Now that your domain is verified, it’s time to get that code onto your site.

<Steps><Step title="Create Your Pixel">Click **Create Your First Pixel** (or **Create Pixel** in the top right). Give it a name—like "Main Website"—and click **Create**.</Step><Step title="Copy the Code">You’ll see a snippet of code under "Copy and paste this script in your web head tracking code." Click the **Click to copy** button.</Step><Step title="Install on Your Website">Go to your website builder (GoHighLevel, WordPress, Webflow, etc.) and find the **Head tracking code** section. Paste the snippet there and hit **Save**.</Step></Steps>

<Tip>If you're not sure where your "Head tracking code" section is, a quick Google search for "[Your Platform Name] head tracking code" will show you exactly where to paste it. The concept is the same across all platforms!</Tip>

Once that's saved, your pixel is live and you're ready to start tracking with total accuracy.
