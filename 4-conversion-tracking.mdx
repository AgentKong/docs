# Meta Lead Form Setup

Alright, so we’re going to walk through how to set up Meta Lead Forms as a conversion source. It’s a bit more "complicated" than other sources, but that’s really just because of how Meta handles permissions. Once you get the handshake right, it works like a charm.

## 1. Connect Your Meta Pages

Meta Lead Forms have their own dedicated tab because there are a few extra steps to get them synced up.

<Steps><Step title="Navigate to Meta Lead Forms">Go to **Conversion Setup** and click the **Meta Lead Forms** tab.</Step><Step title="Select Your Pages">Click the **All Pages** button to see a list of every Facebook page you have access to. Toggle the switch to **On** for any page you want to track.</Step><Step title="Activate Specific Forms">Once a page is connected, you'll see a list of its lead forms. By default, most of these will be turned on, but you can manually toggle individual forms on or off depending on what you want us to track.</Step></Steps>

## 2. Map Your Fields

By default, we automatically map the basics for you: **Email**, **Full Name**, and **Phone Number**. You don't have to lift a finger for those.

<Tip>If you want to track extra data—like custom questions about their industry or budget—you can click **Map Fields**. You can map these to our **Contact Custom Fields**, which is especially useful if you’re using AI Agents that need that context to handle follow-ups.</Tip>

## 3. The Meta "Handshake" (Important!)

Meta is notoriously picky with permissions. Even if everything looks right in our dashboard, you **must** verify the lead access permissions inside your Facebook Business Suite.

1. Go to [**business.facebook.com/settings**](https://www.google.com/url?sa=E&q=https%3A%2F%2Fbusiness.facebook.com%2Fsettings).
2. On the left-hand sidebar, go to **Integrations** > **Lead Access**.
3. Click on the page you’re tracking.
4. Click on the **CRMs** tab.
5. If you don’t see **Cortana AI** listed, click **Assign CRM**, search for it, and hit **Confirm**.

<Warning>If Cortana AI isn't assigned as the CRM for Lead Access inside Facebook, Meta will block us from pulling your leads. Always double-check this step!</Warning>

## 4. Finalize the Conversion Source

Now that the plumbing is set up, we just need to tell the system which "bucket" to put these leads in.

1. Go back to the **Conversion Events** tab.
2. Find your **Lead** event (or whichever event you use for new leads) and click the edit icon.
3. Click **Add Source**.
4. Select **Meta Ads** as the integration and **Lead Form Submissions** as the source.
5. Select your Ad Account and hit **Submit**.

That’s it! Now, every time a lead form fires on Facebook or Instagram, that data will flow right into our system and be attributed to the correct campaign. If you run into any permission snags, just reach out to the support team in the bottom right.

# Webhook Conversion Sources

Alright, to set up a conversion event via a Webhook source, we’re going to dive into the event settings and get that data flowing from your external platforms.

## 1. Create the Webhook Source

First, find the event you want to track and click the edit icon.

<Steps><Step title="Add Source">Click the **Add Source** button and select **Webhook** from the integration dropdown.</Step><Step title="Name Your Webhook">Give your webhook a descriptive name. I always like to name it where it’s coming from and what the automation is (e.g., `GHL - Intro Call Automation`). This makes it a lot easier to backtrack if you ever need to troubleshoot the automation later.</Step><Step title="Grab Your URL">Click **Submit**, and you’ll see a blue **Setup Webhook** button. Click that to reveal your unique Webhook URL.</Step></Steps>

## 2. Connect Your External Platform

Copy that URL and paste it into your external automation tool—whether that’s **GoHighLevel**, **Zapier**, **Make.com**, or even directly inside **ClickFunnels**.

<Tip>Once you've pasted the URL in your external tool, make sure the system is "Listening for events" in our dashboard. Trigger a test from your automation platform to send the first "payload" of data to us.</Tip>

## 3. Mapping Your Fields

Once that first test webhook hits our system, the mapping screen will automatically pop up. Now you just need to tell us which data goes where.

<Note>**Pro Tip:** Use the search bar at the top of the mapping list. It’s a lot easier to search for "Email" or "Phone" than sitting there scrolling through a massive list of data.</Note>

- **Essential Fields:** Drag and drop the **Name**, **Email**, and **Phone** fields from the left (your webhook data) to the right (our system fields).
- **Revenue:** If this is a purchase event, map the revenue field so we can calculate your ROI.

### Advanced Mapping & Attribution

If you know what you’re looking at, you can also map attribution data directly through the webhook.

- **UTM Parameters:** Map your Source, Campaign, Medium, and Content.
- **Click IDs:** You can map fbclid (Meta) or gclid (Google) to ensure deep attribution.

*Note: If you already have a pixel on the page, the pixel handles this automatically. Only use these settings if you're performing a more advanced, pixel-less setup.*

### AI Agent Data

If you’re using our AI Agents, you’ll want to map fields like **Company**, **City**, or **Address**. This gives the agent the context it needs to have meaningful conversations with your leads.

## 4. Managing Webhook Logs

If something isn't looking right, or you just want to verify the data, check the **Requests** column in your conversion source list.

<AccordionGroup><Accordion title="Promoting Test Events" icon="circle-check">If you sent a real event while in "Test" mode, you can go into the logs, find that request, and swap the status from **Test** to **Successful** to count it.</Accordion><Accordion title="Re-Mapping Data" icon="route">Mess up a mapping? No worries. Click the **Edit Mapping** button inside any log entry. We save the "Raw Metadata" (the full payload) from every webhook, so you can re-map fields like Email or Name at any time.</Accordion><Accordion title="Troubleshooting Failures" icon="bug">If a webhook shows as **Failed**, it’s usually because the external platform is "shooting blanks." If they send an empty payload without a Name or Email, we can’t identify the contact, and the mapping will fail.</Accordion></AccordionGroup>

Once your mappings are saved, hit **Save Changes** and away you go! Your webhooks are now powering your conversion tracking.
