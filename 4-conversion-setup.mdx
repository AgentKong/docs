# Conversion Event Setup

Alright, now that we have our pixel installed and our contact tracking dialed in, we’re going to head over to the **Conversion Setup** tab. This is where you’ll create, edit, and map data for your conversion events so you know exactly what’s happening in your funnel.

## Getting Started with Events

Right out of the gate, we give you a set of standard events—things like Lead, Intro Call Booked, Appointment Shown, and New Deals Closed. These are the basics, but you aren’t limited to them.

<Tip>You can create **unlimited custom events**. If you want to track a "VIP Webinar Upsell," just click **Add Custom Event**, give it a name, and a description.</Tip>

## Configuring Your Tracking Mode

This is an important one. You have two ways to track how contacts interact with your events:

- **Track all conversions:** This records every single time an event happens for a contact. This is the recommended setting for most setups.
- **Track first only:** This is a hard stop. It will only record the very first time a specific conversion happens for a contact and ignore everything after that.

### Pro Tip: Filtering vs. Tracking

I generally recommend tracking **all** conversions. Why? Because you can always filter the data later in your reports. For example, if someone reschedules an appointment, you might want to track both events but only *show* one unique booking every 14 days in your attribution table to keep your cost-per-booking numbers accurate.

## Revenue Categories

When an event involves money, you need to decide how that revenue is categorized in your dashboard.

<CardGroup cols={2}><Card title="New Revenue" icon="money-bill-wave">Revenue collected from a specific transaction at the time a customer converts (e.g., a Shopify order or the first payment of a subscription).</Card><Card title="LTV (Lifetime Value)" icon="chart-line-up">The total value of all successful payments from a source over time. This helps you track the long-term ROI of your campaigns.</Card></CardGroup>

## Adding Conversion Sources

A conversion event is just a bucket; you need to tell it where the data is coming from. These are your **Conversion Sources**.

<Steps><Step title="Select an Event">Click the edit icon next to any event (like "New Deals Closed").</Step><Step title="Add Source">Click **Add Source** and choose your integration. We support everything from Webhooks and CSV uploads to native integrations like Salesforce and Meta Ads.</Step><Step title="Map the Data">If you’re using Stripe, for example, you can select specific events like "Checkout Completed" and even filter it so it only triggers for specific products.</Step></Steps>

<Note>**Automatic Setup:** When you connect a native payment processor like **Stripe** or **Whop**, we automatically create an "All Payments LTV" event for you. We know the best methodology for tracking this, so we handle the heavy lifting out of the box.</Note>

Our system doesn't really care where the data comes from—you can have multiple sources (like a Webhook and a CRM) all feeding into a single "Intro Call Booked" event. This gives you the "Lego pieces" you need to build a tracking setup that fits your specific business.

If you have any questions on how to map these out, feel free to reach out to the support team in the bottom right!
